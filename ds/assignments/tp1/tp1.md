### 4. In engineering, design is classified along a spectrum that at the two extremes has normal design and radical design

#### a) Explain what the two terms mean

> Normal design follows modern conventions, focusing on functional improvements, efficiency, and mainstream asthetics. Radical design, or anti-design, challenges traditional norms, often prioritizing conceptual statements, emotional impact, and unconventional forms.

#### b) Indicate what the main differentiating aspects are and how they vary between the two ends of this spectrum

> Radical design can provoke thought and challenge societal norms, but it may also alienate users who prefer familiar and functional designs. Normal design tends to be more user-friendly and widely accepted, but it may lack innovation and fail to inspire.

#### c) What are the potential risks and challenges associated with implementing radical design in software engineering

> Implementing a radical design approach in everyday products could lead to unique and thought-provoking items that stand out in the market. However, it may also result in usability issues and limited appeal, as consumers often prioritize practicality and comfort in their daily lives. In a software engineering setting, radical design could foster creativity and innovation, but it might also complicate user experience and hinder adoption.

#### d) Give an example of a software system that illustrates each end of the spectrum. Justify your choices

For each end of the spectrum, I would choose the following products:

- **Normal Design:** A smartphone, as it requires a balance of functionality, user-friendliness, and aesthetic appeal to cater to a broad audience.
- **Radical Design:** A conceptual art installation, as it allows for creative freedom and the opportunity to challenge traditional design norms without the constraints of practicality.

#### e) In various articles and presentations, M. Jackson argues that much (if not most) of software development involves radical design

##### i) Give at least 2 reasons why this might be the case

> Software design is more often radical because it allows for greater experimentation and innovation in user interfaces, interactions, and functionalities. Software can be updated and modified more easily than physical products, enabling designers to explore unconventional ideas without the limitations of manufacturing and material constraints.

##### ii) To what extent specialization, according to multiple dimensions, but especially specialization in artefacts (which the author advocates) can help change the current panorama

> Radical design helps change the panorama with new perspectives and innovative solutions that challenge existing norms. It encourages designers to think outside the box, leading to breakthroughs in user experience and functionality that can redefine how users interact with technology.
